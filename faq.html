Frequently asked questions

Everything people usually want to know about Bloop Maker, the game music generator that uses music theory instead of AI.

Does Bloop Maker use AI?

No. It generates music using music-theory rules written in code: chord progressions, melody logic, motif repetition, and drum patterns. No machine learning, no training data, nothing scraped. It composes the way a game generates a level: from rules, not from a model.

Where do the rules come from, if not AI?

Two places: established music theory (chords, scales, melody, resolving tension), and the conventions of game music itself. The key difference from AI is that Bloop Maker is rule-based and deterministic, not a model trained on songs. Full explanation here.

Is the music royalty-free and safe to use commercially?

Yes. The music is built by code and not sampled from existing recordings, so it's yours, free and clear, for commercial games, monetized videos, streams, trailers, jams, and personal projects. No copyright-claim risk, and nothing for a platform to flag as AI content.

Does it need the internet?

No. After download it runs completely offline. No account, no subscription, no cloud. Your songs stay on your computer.

What can I export?

You can export standard MIDI files that open in any DAW (FL Studio, Ableton, LMMS, Reaper, and others), WAV audio rendered with your own soundfonts, individual channels on their own, and full project files you can reopen and keep editing later.

What do I need to hear sound?

A free SoundFont (.sf2) file. A SoundFont holds the instrument sounds, telling the app what each note should sound like. Without one you can still generate songs and export MIDI; you just won't hear it play inside the app.

How do I use a SoundFont, and where can I get one?

To use a SoundFont, just drag a .sf2 file into the Soundfont box in Bloop Maker (or click the box to browse for it). Once it loads, press play and you'll hear your song through those instruments. You can swap soundfonts at any time, and in the full version you can even assign a different one to each channel.

Some good free SoundFonts to start with:

  • Arachno SoundFont (General MIDI, Roland GS)
  • GeneralUser GS (General MIDI, Roland GS)
  • Roland SC-55 (classic game-console sound)

You can also browse many more on Musical Artifacts and Soundfonts 4U.

Do I need a license for the SoundFont I use?

Worth understanding before you publish. The music itself (the composition) is yours. But a SoundFont is a separate product made by someone else, with its own license, and Bloop Maker does not grant you rights to it. What that means:

  • Exporting MIDI is always safe. A MIDI file is just notes, no soundfont sounds, so there's no soundfont license to worry about.
  • Exporting WAV bakes the soundfont's sounds into the file. The instrument samples become part of the audio, so the soundfont's license then applies to it.

So before using rendered audio in a commercial project, check the license of the SoundFont you used and make sure commercial use is allowed. Licenses vary: some allow any use, some are personal-only, some require credit. Most soundfonts include a license file, read it. The song is yours; the sound belongs to whoever made the soundfont.

Do I need to know music theory?

Not at all. Pick a mood and press Generate to get a finished song, the theory runs automatically under the hood. If you do know theory, there are deeper controls for scale, key, melodic archetype, arpeggio style, and arrangement.

How is this different from an AI music generator?

AI tools are trained on existing songs and predict what comes next and they usually need an account, the cloud, a subscription, or a wait in a queue. Bloop Maker is the opposite: rule-based and deterministic, fully offline, instant with no waiting, and the music is yours outright, the same song every time from the same seed.

Yes. Every song opens in a full piano-roll editor where you can add, remove, and resize individual notes. You can also reshape parts with one-click tools, regenerate any single channel while keeping the rest, and undo anything.

My songs don't sound that good. How do I make them better?

Honesty first: Bloop Maker is a tool to spark ideas and get unstuck, not a one-click machine for finished, award winning tracks. It's a creative co-pilot, best when your brain isn't cooperating and you just need a starting point: a hook, a chord progression, a vibe. Some rolls are gold, some are junk, that's normal. Find the spark, then build on it.

With that said, here is how to get much better results out of it:

  • Generate a few times. It's instant and unlimited, so roll several songs and keep the ones with a hook you like. Copy the seed of a good one so you can always get it back.
  • Use the steering buttons. Bright, Focused, Cinematic, Lush, Punchy, and Lo-fi completely change the character. "Focused" in particular makes the melody more repetitive and catchy, which usually sounds more like a real song.
  • Try a different SoundFont. The same notes sound completely different through different instruments. Experiment with soundfonts until the sound fits the mood.
  • Trim the clutter. If it sounds busy or chaotic, mute channels you don't need, press Simplify on a part, or raise the sparseness so instruments rest more and the melody can breathe.
  • Fix the parts you don't like. Regenerate a single channel while keeping the rest, or open the piano roll and nudge individual notes. Often one weird note is all that's holding a song back.
  • Match the mood. Pick a key, scale, and mode that fit what you're going for. Minor scales feel darker, major and lydian feel brighter, and the tempo changes the energy a lot.
  • Lock a hook. If you find a melodic shape you love, lock a motif or archetype so the hook repeats through the song instead of wandering.
  • Finish it in a DAW. This is the big one. Export the MIDI, drop it into FL Studio, Ableton, LMMS, Reaper, or any DAW, swap in your own instruments, add effects, and arrange it. Bloop Maker gives you the skeleton and sometimes the full body but the best results almost always come from taking an idea out of Bloop Maker and finishing it with your own touch.

Treat it as a brainstorming partner. You probably won't get a masterpiece straight out of it, but you will very often get a melody, a progression, or a feeling that unsticks you and steers you somewhere good. That little creative boost, on the days you need it most, is exactly what it is built for.

Can it make music other than game music?

It is tuned for chiptune and video game music, but its modes and scales cover a wide range of moods, from calm and ambient to jazzy, funky, and intense. Since it exports standard MIDI, you can take that anywhere and turn it into any style in your own DAW.

What is a SoundFont?

A SoundFont (.sf2) is a standard file that holds instrument sounds, telling the app what each note should sound like. Bloop Maker uses whichever SoundFont you load, so you can give your songs anything from retro bleeps to a full orchestra. Plenty of high-quality SoundFonts are free to download.
