Frequently asked questions

Everything people usually want to know about Bloop Maker, the game music generator that uses music theory instead of AI.

Does Bloop Maker use AI?

No. Bloop Maker generates music using music-theory rules written in code, chord progressions, melody logic, motif repetition, harmonic rhythm, and drum patterns. There is no machine learning, no neural network, no training data, and nothing scraped from other people's music. It composes the way a game procedurally generates a level: from rules, not from a model.

Where do the rules come from, if not AI?

Two places. First, established music theory, the same principles taught in any music classroom about chords, scales, melody, and resolving tension. Second, the conventions of game music itself: the generator leans into the melodic, harmonic, and rhythmic patterns the genre is known for, expressed as weighted choices. The crucial difference from AI is that Bloop Maker is rule-based and deterministic rather than a model trained on songs, so the same input always gives the same output and nothing is hidden inside a black box. Is the music royalty-free and safe to use commercially?

Yes. Because the music is built by deterministic code and not sampled from existing recordings, the output is yours, free and clear. Use it in commercial games, monetized YouTube videos, Twitch streams, trailers, game jam entries, apps, and personal projects. There is no copyright-claim risk, and no platform can flag it as AI-generated content because it isn't.

Does it need the internet?

No. After download, Bloop Maker runs completely offline. No account, no subscription, no cloud, no waiting room. Your songs stay on your own computer.

What can I export?

You can export standard MIDI files that open in any DAW (FL Studio, Ableton, LMMS, Reaper, and others), WAV audio rendered with your own soundfonts, individual channels on their own, and full project files you can reopen and keep editing later.

What do I need to hear sound?

A free SoundFont (.sf2) file. Search for "GeneralUser GS sf2" or "FluidR3 GM sf2", both are free. Drag it into Bloop Maker and you'll have instruments. You can even load several soundfonts and assign different ones to different channels. Without a soundfont you can still generate and export MIDI; you just won't hear it play inside the app.

What's limited in the free demo?

The demo is a real taste of the full app. You can generate, edit, steer, and hear Bloop Maker, but it's limited to 4 channels, a small set of presets, arpeggio styles, and archetypes, shorter songs, and no file export. The full version unlocks all six-plus channels, every option, longer songs, and MIDI + WAV export.

Why does my computer warn that the app is unsigned?

Code-signing certificates are expensive for solo developers and tiny software shops, so the builds aren't signed yet. On macOS, right-click the app and choose Open. On Windows, click More info then Run anyway. This is completely normal for indie tools, the app is safe, offline, and never sends your work anywhere.

Do I need to know music theory?

Not at all. Pick a mood and press Generate to get a finished song, the theory runs automatically under the hood. If you do know theory, there are deeper controls for scale, key, melodic archetype, arpeggio style, and arrangement.

What platforms does it run on?

Windows, macOS, and Linux. It's a desktop app you download and own.

Will the music get a copyright claim on YouTube or Twitch?

It shouldn't. The music is original and generated by deterministic rules, not sampled from any recording and not produced by a model trained on other people's songs. There is no existing rights-holder to claim it, so it is safe for monetized YouTube videos, Twitch streams, and other platforms.

Can I sell a game that uses Bloop Maker music?

Yes. The music you generate is yours to use in commercial projects, including paid games, apps, and videos. It is a one-time purchase with no per-use fees and no royalties.

How is this different from an AI music generator?

AI music tools are trained on large collections of existing songs and predict what comes next, which raises copyright and "is this AI content?" questions, and they often need an account, the cloud, or a subscription. Bloop Maker is the opposite: it is rule-based and deterministic, runs fully offline, produces original music you own outright, and gives the same song every time from the same seed.

Can I edit the songs after generating them?

Yes. Every song opens in a full piano-roll editor where you can add, remove, and resize individual notes. You can also reshape parts with one-click tools, regenerate any single channel while keeping the rest, and undo anything.

Can it make music other than game music?

It is tuned for chiptune and video game music, but its modes and scales cover a wide range of moods, from calm and ambient to jazzy, funky, and intense. Since it exports standard MIDI, you can take that anywhere and turn it into any style in your own DAW.

What is a SoundFont?

A SoundFont (.sf2) is a standard file that holds instrument sounds, telling the app what each note should sound like. Bloop Maker uses whichever SoundFont you load, so you can give your songs anything from retro bleeps to a full orchestra. Plenty of high-quality SoundFonts are free to download.
