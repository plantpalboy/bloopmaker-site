What it is & how it works

What Bloop Maker is

Bloop Maker is a desktop app that composes original video game music. You set the rules, press one button, and it writes a complete multi-track song. You can edit every note, shape the feel, and export the result as a MIDI file or finished audio. It runs entirely on your own computer, works offline, and uses no AI and no samples, so every song is yours to use, with no copyright strings attached.

How it works behind the scenes

Bloop Maker doesn't guess or imitate other music. It builds songs from music-theory rules written in code, step by step, the way a game procedurally builds a level:

  • Seed. Every song starts from a seed number. The same seed always produces the exact same song, so you can save and share them.
  • Chords. It chooses a key and scale, then builds a chord progression from music-theory tables. Two progressions alternate to keep things moving.
  • Motif. It generates a short, catchy melodic "hook" of a few notes, scores it for quality, and regenerates it until it's strong.
  • Signature rhythm. It commits to one rhythmic pattern used across the whole song, so the melody feels consistent instead of random.
  • Phrase memory. The first few bars are remembered and replayed (in key with the current chord) in later sections, with small variations each time, that's what gives a song a recognizable hook instead of aimless notes.
  • Structure. It plans an intro, verse, chorus, break, and outro, changing the energy, pitch range, and bass behavior in each section.
  • Filling the bars. Melody, harmony, bass, drums, and pad are written bar by bar, snapping key notes to chord tones, recovering from big leaps, and adding ornaments, suspensions, call-and-response, and countermelody.
  • Polish. Final passes lock strong beats to chord tones, sprinkle in chromatic approach notes and borrowed chords for color, and adds drum fills before section changes.

FAQ

Does Bloop Maker use AI?

No. Every song is generated by music-theory rules written in code, chord progressions, melody logic, motif repetition, and drum patterns. There's no machine learning, no training data, and nothing scraped from other music.

Is the music safe to use commercially?

Yes. Because the music is built by code and not sampled from anything, it's yours free and clear. Use it in commercial games, monetized videos, live streams, trailers, and personal projects without copyright worries.

Does it need the internet?

No. After you download it, Bloop Maker runs completely offline. No account, no cloud, no data leaves your computer.

What do I need to hear sound?

A free SoundFont file (.sf2), search "GeneralUser GS sf2" or "FluidR3 GM sf2". Drag it in and you'll hear audio. Without one you can still generate and export MIDI but you won't hear it play inside the app.

What can I export?

Standard MIDI files that open in any DAW (FL Studio, Ableton, LMMS, Reaper, and more), WAV audio rendered with your soundfont, individual channels on their own, and full project files you can reopen later.

What's different in the free demo?

The demo lets you fully experience generating, editing, and hearing Bloop Maker, but it's limited to 4 channels, a few presets, arps, and archetypes, shorter songs, and no file export. The full version unlocks everything and lets you save and export your music.

What platforms does it run on?

Windows, macOS, and Linux.
