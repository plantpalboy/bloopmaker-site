What it is & how it works

What Bloop Maker is

Bloop Maker is a desktop app that composes original video game music. you pick a vibe, press one button, and it writes you a whole multi-track song. then you can edit every note, shape the feel, and export it as a MIDI file or finished audio. it runs entirely on your own computer, works offline, and uses no AI and no samples, so every song is yours to use, with no copyright strings attached.

How it works behind the scenes

Bloop Maker doesn't guess or imitate other music. it builds songs from music-theory rules written in code, step by step, the way a game procedurally builds a level:

  • Seed. every song starts from a seed number. the same seed always gives you the exact same song, so you can save them and share them.
  • Chords. it picks a key and scale, then builds a chord progression from music-theory tables. two progressions trade off to keep things moving.
  • Motif. it writes a short, catchy little hook of a few notes, scores it for quality, and writes it again until it's strong.
  • Signature rhythm. it commits to one rhythmic pattern and uses it across the whole song, so the melody feels consistent instead of random.
  • Phrase memory. the first few bars get remembered and replayed (in key with the current chord) in later sections, with small changes each time, that's what gives a song a recognizable hook instead of notes wandering off.
  • Structure. it plans an intro, verse, chorus, break, and outro, shifting the energy, pitch range, and bass behavior in each section.
  • Filling the bars. melody, harmony, bass, drums, and pad get written bar by bar, snapping key notes onto chord tones, recovering from big leaps, and adding ornaments, suspensions, call-and-response, and countermelody.
  • Polish. final passes lock strong beats to chord tones, sprinkle in chromatic approach notes and borrowed chords for color, add drum fills before section changes, and shape a dynamics curve so the song breathes.

FAQ

Does Bloop Maker use AI?

no. every song is made by music-theory rules written in code, chord progressions, melody logic, motif repetition, drum patterns. no machine learning, no training data, nothing scraped from anyone else's music.

Is the music safe to use commercially?

yes. because the music is built by code and not sampled from anything, it's yours free and clear. use it in commercial games, monetized videos, live streams, trailers, and personal projects, no copyright worries.

Does it need the internet?

no. once you've downloaded it, Bloop Maker runs completely offline. no account, no cloud, and nothing leaves your computer.

What do I need to hear sound?

a free SoundFont file (.sf2), search "GeneralUser GS sf2" or "FluidR3 GM sf2". drag it in and you'll hear audio. without one you can still generate songs and export MIDI, you just won't hear it play inside the app.

What can I export?

standard MIDI files that open in any DAW (FL Studio, Ableton, LMMS, Reaper, and more), WAV audio rendered with your soundfont, individual channels on their own, and full project files you can reopen later.

What's different in the free demo?

the demo lets you fully feel what it's like to generate, edit, and hear Bloop Maker, it's just limited to 4 channels, a few presets, arps, and archetypes, shorter songs, and no file export. the full version unlocks everything and lets you save and export your music.

Why does my computer warn the app is unsigned?

code-signing certificates are expensive for solo developers, so the builds aren't signed. on macOS, right-click the app and choose Open. on Windows, click "More info" then "Run anyway". this is normal for indie tools, the app is safe and offline.

What platforms does it run on?

Windows, macOS, and Linux.
